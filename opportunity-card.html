How it works

The Point-Based System

The Opportunity Card operates on a transparent point system. You need to score a minimum of 6 points to qualify for the residence permit.

2 Age < 35
1 Age 35-40
3 B1 German
2 A2 German
1 A1 German
1 B2 English

Core Requirements

Recognized Qualification

Professional or academic qualification recognized in your country of origin (min. 2 years training).

Language or Experience

Sufficient German (A1) or English language skills (B2) are foundational for your job search.

Financial Means

Proof of approx. €1,091 per month through a blocked account or a legal sponsorship letter (Verpflichtungserklärung).

Pro Tip: Having a degree fully recognized in Germany often bypasses the point system entirely! Contact our experts for a free assessment of your credentials.

Journey Map

Pathway to Residency

Your step-by-step roadmap from initial application to permanent German settlement.

Phase 1: Application

Secure your Opportunity Card by meeting the points criteria. Processing typically takes 6-12 weeks.

Phase 2: Relocation

Arrive in Germany. You can live anywhere and start your job search while working up to 20hrs/week.

Phase 3: Career Match

Land a full-time position matching your qualifications with a starting salary above the required threshold.

Phase 4: Status Upgrade

Switch your permit to an EU Blue Card or a standard Work Residence Permit without leaving Germany.

Phase 5: Settlement (PR)

Apply for Permanent Residence after 21-33 months of working and contributing to social security.
